Three-piece sterile hypodermic syringe for single use with «Luer» tip of capacity 1cc with a single-use hypodermic needle

Three-piece sterile hypodermic syringe for single use with «Luer» tip of capacity 1cc with a single-use hypodermic needle complies with the ISO 7886-1 standard.

The syringe includes a cylinder, a piston with a seal and a single-use injection needle worn with a safety cap. The calibration of the scale is 0.05 cc. The locking ring on the inner surface of the cylinder prevents accidental pulling of the piston at the time of the drug intake.

The piston seal (cuff) is treated with silicone grease for smooth and uniform movement of the piston. The cuff has rings for contact with the inner surface of the cylinder, which eliminates leakage.

The syringe is equipped with a needle (put on) or single-use injection needles with a standard cut, with a triangular sharpening according to ISO 7864. The outer surface of the injection needle tube is light, smooth, without scratches, cracks, shells, nicks, crumbled places, burrs, delaminations and other visible defects.

The size of the needle is 27G (0.4x12 mm). The color coding of the needle meets the requirements of the ISO 6009 standard. The material of the needle cannula is stainless steel (SUS304). The safety cap of the needle has four stiffeners that provide ease of removal, and, accordingly, additional safety of medical personnel when removing the cap from the needle and reduces the risk of contamination of the needle when removing the cap, and also saves time for performing a simple medical manipulation "injection". Individual sterile blister packaging – dense gas-permeable paper and transparent film (the composition of the film is polyamide).

The package is opened with little effort, which prevents spontaneous opening, without the formation of a paper fringe. Sterile, non-toxic, non-pyrogenic. Sterilization is carried out with ethylene oxide in accordance with EN ISO 11135-1. The shelf life is 5 years. The monthly throughput is more than 8 million units.
